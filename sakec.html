About SDG 15 – Life on Land

SDG 15 aims to protect, restore and promote sustainable use of terrestrial ecosystems. It focuses on forests, land, soil, biodiversity, and wildlife preservation.

Why SDG 15 is Important

Land ecosystems provide food, water, oxygen, medicines, building materials and carbon absorption. Without forests and biodiversity, life on Earth becomes unstable.

Key Targets of SDG 15

  • Restore degraded forests and drylands
  • Improve land productivity
  • Protect mountains and freshwater ecosystems
  • Reduce poaching and eliminate illegal wildlife trade
  • Increase global conservation funding

Facts

  • Over 75% of land is degraded globally.
  • Forests absorb 2 billion tons of CO₂ annually.
  • 12 million hectares of land are lost every year due to deforestation.

Biodiversity & Threats

Biodiversity refers to the variety of life on Earth. Healthy ecosystems depend on diverse species to function properly.

Main Threats to Biodiversity

  • Deforestation: Clearing forests for agriculture and construction.
  • Pollution: Air, water, and soil pollution destroy habitats.
  • Climate Change: Rising temperatures disrupt ecosystems.
  • Poaching: Illegal hunting and wildlife trade.
  • Habitat Fragmentation: Roads, buildings split animal habitats.

Impact of Biodiversity Loss

  • Loss of medicinal plants and raw materials
  • Increase in natural disasters
  • Reduction of pollinators → food shortage
  • Soil erosion and land degradation

Global Efforts to Protect Land & Forests

Major International Programs

  • UN REDD+ Program: Reducing emissions from deforestation.
  • UN Convention on Biological Diversity: Global preservation of species.
  • IUCN Red List: Tracks endangered species worldwide.
  • Global Forest Watch: Tracks forest loss using satellites.

Success Stories

  • India increased tiger population by 30% in 4 years.
  • Costa Rica restored 50% of its forests through policy reforms.
  • China planted the largest manmade forest to fight desertification.

Challenges

  • Lack of funding
  • Weak enforcement of laws
  • Poverty-driven habitat destruction
